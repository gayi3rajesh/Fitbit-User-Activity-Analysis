Introduction

This report analyzes personal fitness tracker data from 35 Fitbit users. The goal is to explore users’ habits through visualizations and statistical analysis. The dataset contains daily activity, steps, heart rate, and calories burned.

Conclusion

From the visualizations, we observe:

  1. Most users fall into the “Moderate” and “very active” activity levels.
  2. There is a positive correlation between the number of steps taken and calories burned.
  3. Calorie expenditure varies significantly with activity level, with “Very Active” users burning the most.

Further analysis could include clustering users based on activity patterns and exploring seasonal variations in activity levels.
